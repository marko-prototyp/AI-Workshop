---
num: "02"
title: "Research & synthesis"
phase: "Discover"
capability: "Claude as research partner; structured comparison; challenging assumptions"
demo: "Show the difference between a generic 'what makes a good portfolio?' question and a structured comparison of 5 specific portfolios you name, by criteria you set, in a table."
exercise:
  - "Find 5–10 reference examples"
  - "Build a comparison matrix in Claude"
  - "Have Claude challenge your project's assumptions"
  - "Synthesize into a one-page summary"
prompts:
  - title: "Find the references for me"
    body: |
      I'm looking for references for [project type]. The kind of inspiration I want is [be concrete. Name the qualities, the feeling, what it should and shouldn't do]. Go search the web. Bring back 8 to 10 specific examples with URLs. For each one: what it does well, what's distinctive about it, one specific move I could borrow. Skip generic results. If nothing fits the brief, say so and ask me to sharpen the criteria before searching again.
  - title: "Structured comparison"
    body: |
      I'm researching [project type]. Here are 5 examples I've found inspiring: [list with URLs]. Build me a comparison table with columns: name, what it does well, what's distinctive, what NOT to copy, one specific technique I could borrow. Be specific — vague answers are useless.
  - title: "Write the project brief"
    body: |
      Take everything we've covered so far. References, comparison, what I've told you about the project. Turn it into a project brief I can save as a markdown file. Structure: what I'm building in one sentence, who it's for, what success looks like, the wanted features in priority order, the research points and what they taught me, what I'm explicitly not building. Be specific where I've been specific. Where I've been vague, ask me one question at a time to sharpen it before writing that section. The brief is what we come back to every session. Don't summarise loosely.
  - title: "Challenge assumptions"
    body: |
      Here's my project brief: [paste]. Without being polite, push back on it. What assumptions am I making that might be wrong? What's the strongest case for doing the opposite of what I've planned?
outputs:
  - "Comparison matrix"
  - "Pushed-back assumptions"
  - "One-page research summary"
watch:
  - "Don't let people skip naming specific examples"
  - "Synthesis is harder than research; budget extra time"
---
