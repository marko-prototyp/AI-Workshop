title: "Tear it apart."
phase: "Polish"
capability: "AI as critical eye; accessibility; consistency; anti-stock review"
demo: "Critique a volunteer's project across multiple lenses: accessibility, hierarchy, copy, consistency, stock-UI drift. Show how to ask for specific, actionable critique."
exercise:
  - "Run the project through 5 critique lenses"
  - "Make a prioritized fix list"
  - "Start fixing must-fixes during the session"
prompts:
  - title: "Multi-lens critique"
    body: |
      Critique my project across 5 lenses, one at a time:
      1. Accessibility — contrast, focus, semantic HTML, alt, keyboard
      2. Visual hierarchy — what does the eye go to?
      3. Copy — does it sound human? Reference my banned-words: [paste]
      4. Consistency — spacing, type, alignment
      5. Stock-UI drift — does it feel generic? Compare against my Figma comps.

      For each, give me 3 specific issues with locations, ranked. Only what to fix.
  - title: "Skeptical persona"
    body: |
      Pretend you're [persona — e.g., a hiring designer at a top studio with 30 seconds]. Walk through my project. What do you notice in 5 seconds? Where do you lose interest? Bookmark or close?
outputs:
  - "A 5-lens critique"
  - "A prioritized fix list"
  - "2+ must-fixes addressed in session"
watch:
  - "Critique stings — Claude is wrong sometimes; designer filters"
  - "Don't let people fix easy things first; must-fixes are usually harder"
