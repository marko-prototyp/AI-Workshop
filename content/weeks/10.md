---
num: "10"
title: "Find what's wrong. Review it hard."
phase: "Ship"
principle: "The model is a sharper critic than a cheerleader. Ask it to break your work, not praise it, and the review changes what you ship."
capability: "Hostile QA; designed failure states; multi-lens critique; impact-ranked fix list"
aim: "Look at your project through the harshest lens you can, then turn the breakage into three fixes you'll actually make."
toolkit: "Opus in Claude chat"
tools:
  - Opus in Claude chat
flow:
  - Break it
  - Critique it
  - Rank to three
before: "Use Opus for this one. The depth of the critique decides what gets fixed, and the stronger model finds more."
exercise:
  - "Run hostile QA on your own project and design one honest failure state."
  - "Run the six-lens critique pass."
  - "Rank everything down to your top three fixes."
prompts:
  - title: "Find the breaks"
    tool: "Opus in Claude chat"
    does: "Hand your project to a hostile tester and let it list every way it falls over."
    preamble: "Who's the worst user of your app: slow connection, wrong locale, wrong file, hostile input? Predict three breaks before you read its list."
    body: |
      Pretend you're a hostile QA tester. Walk through my app [point Code at it, or describe the flow] and list every way you could break it: bad input, slow network, wrong file format, empty state, no response, weird locale, unusual screen size, anything. Rank by how likely it is in real use, not by severity. For each, name what would visibly happen right now, the unhandled case.
    note: "Pick one break worth designing for and build its failure state honestly: \"For [break case], design the failure state. The user should know what failed and what they can do. Don't make it apologetic. Match my [project]-design skill, then slot it in where the failure actually happens.\""
    gives: "Every way your project breaks, ranked by likelihood, and at least one honest failure state."
  - title: "Critique across six lenses"
    tool: "Opus in Claude chat"
    does: "Run six critique lenses, one at a time. The one you'd rather not ask is usually the one that matters."
    preamble: "What's the question about your project you've been avoiding?"
    body: |
      Critique my project across six lenses, one at a time:
      1. Accessibility: contrast, focus, semantic HTML, alt text, keyboard navigation.
      2. Visual hierarchy: what does the eye go to, what gets lost?
      3. Copy: does it sound human? Check it against the banned words in my skill.
      4. Consistency: spacing, type, alignment, state transitions.
      5. Stock-UI drift: does it feel generic? Compare against my main view.
      6. Device fit: does it work on the device my real user actually has?

      For each, give me three specific issues with locations, ranked by severity. Only what to fix, not what's already good.
    gives: "Located issues across access, hierarchy, copy, consistency, drift, and device."
  - title: "Rank to three"
    tool: "Opus in Claude chat"
    does: "Converge the break list and the critique into the three fixes that change the project most."
    preamble: "Not the easy three. The three that matter. Which ones?"
    body: |
      Of everything you raised across the breaks and the six lenses, which three would have the largest visible impact if I fixed them in the next 30 minutes? Don't pick the easy ones. Pick the ones that change my project most. For each: name the issue, name the fix, estimate the time.
    gives: "Three fixes that change the project most. Your must-fix list for ship."
outputs:
  - "A hostile QA break list."
  - "At least one honest failure state."
  - "A six-lens critique with locations."
  - "Your top three fixes."
watch:
  - "Don't fix the easy things first. The must-fixes are usually harder."
  - "Device fit catches what the laptop never showed. Test on the real phone."
  - "Carry three fixes into ship, not twenty."
---
