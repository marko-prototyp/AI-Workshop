---
num: "08"
title: "Secondary views. Real states."
phase: "Make"
principle: "Your real user meets the empty screen, the spinner, and the error before the happy path. Design those states, because the model won't unless you ask."
capability: "State mapping; reference-led building; routing; persistence"
aim: "Build every other screen from your skill and your main view, and design the states everyone forgets: empty, loading, error."
toolkit: "Claude chat · Claude Code"
tools:
  - Claude chat
  - Claude Code
flow:
  - List the states
  - Build and route
  - Wire one interaction
before: "Your design system, your [project]-design skill, and your built main view all live in your project. You build the rest of the screens from them, so you never re-supply the look."
exercise:
  - "List every state every screen can be in, including the ones usually skipped."
  - "Build each screen from a reference and route between them, one section at a time."
  - "Wire the one interaction that matters most."
  - "Optional: persist anything that should survive a reload."
prompts:
  - title: "List every state"
    tool: "Claude chat"
    does: "Most screens get the happy path designed and nothing else. List every state first, before you build."
    preamble: "What does your screen look like with nothing loaded? With everything broken?"
    body: |
      For my [screen name], list every state it can be in: default, loading, empty, error, success, hovered, focused, and anything specific to this screen. For each: what's visible, what's hidden, and how the user moves between them. Don't build anything yet, just the list. At the end, tell me which states are most likely to get skipped and why I shouldn't skip them.
    gives: "A full state list per screen, including the ones usually skipped."
  - title: "Build the screen from a reference, then route"
    tool: "Claude Code"
    does: "Build the screen against a reference, one section at a time, then connect it to the others. Your skill carries the look."
    preamble: "What's the closest reference: your built main view, or a quick Figma sketch? Words drift, images don't."
    body: |
      Build [screen name] for [project]. Use my [project]-design skill for the look and the tokens, and keep it consistent with my main view.

      Reference: [attach a quick Figma sketch, reference screenshots, or point to your built main view].
      What it shows and does: [describe it, from your W04 wireframe and W05 content].
      States to cover: [from your state list].

      Build the layout first, one section at a time. Then add the routing: how the user gets here and back. If the screen needs a pattern that's nowhere in my skill or main view yet, ask before inventing one.
    note: "One section per prompt, never the whole page. Don't normalise unusual choices in the reference. Treat them as intentional."
    gives: "The screen built to match its reference and your skill, reachable from the rest of the app."
  - title: "Wire one interaction"
    tool: "Claude Code"
    does: "Wire the one interaction that matters most on this screen. Describe it in plain English before any code."
    preamble: "Can you describe the interaction to a non-engineer in plain English? If you can't, Code won't get it right either."
    body: |
      I want [interaction]. Here are the two states: [attach 'before'] and [attach 'after'].

      Describe step by step what should happen between them in plain English first, not code. Include: what triggers it, what animates, what stays still, what happens if I trigger it twice fast, and what happens if the user closes the tab mid-interaction. After I confirm, build it in vanilla JS to match my project's character.
    note: "If something breaks: \"After the last change, [thing] stopped working. Don't rebuild the whole screen. Find what broke and patch only that. Show me the smallest fix.\""
    gives: "One real interaction working."
  - title: "Make it stick"
    tool: "Claude Code"
    does: "Optional. Decide what should survive a reload. That's what localStorage is for. One user, no backend."
    preamble: "What in your project would be infuriating to lose on a refresh? Start there."
    body: |
      For each screen with user-generated data, decide: does it need to survive a page reload? If yes, walk me through saving to and reading from localStorage. Show me the keys, what the empty state looks like before anything's saved, and what happens if the saved data is corrupted or partial. Treat me like someone who's never written JavaScript persistence. After it works, tell me what would break if I cleared my browser cache and how to handle it.
    note: "Optional. localStorage is enough for one-user projects. No backend needed."
    gives: "Anything that should survive a reload now does."
outputs:
  - "All screens built and routed."
  - "Real empty, loading, and error states."
  - "One interaction wired."
  - "Optional: state that survives a reload."
watch:
  - "Loading and empty states get skipped most. Insist on at least one."
  - "Your skill carries the look and the banned moves. Point Code at it, don't re-paste them."
  - "One section per prompt. Building a whole screen at once is how drift creeps back."
---
