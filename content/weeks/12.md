---
num: "12"
title: "Polish. Ship. Show."
phase: "Ship → Done"
capability: "Polish discipline; deploy via GitHub integration; presentation thinking"
aim: "Polish is what you remove, not what you add. What's the one 'just in case' thing in your project you could delete right now?"
toolkit: "Sonnet, Cowork or Claude Code for deploy. Polish, ship, and talk drafting are mechanical-shaped; Sonnet's speed helps."
exercise:
  - "First 25 min: polish — fixes only, from the W11 must-fix list"
  - "Next 10 min: connect repo to Vercel, push, get a live URL, env vars for API keys"
  - "Last 25 min: 5-minute show & tell per person plus a short retro"
prompts:
  - title: "Last polish pass"
    preamble: "What does your project still need? The honest answer is usually shorter than the panicked one."
    body: |
      I have 25 minutes for final polish. Walk through my project [link or paste] and give me fixes ranked by impact per minute. Stop me if I drift toward features. The list should end at the 25-minute mark, and the last item is the one I'd be most embarrassed to ship without.
  - title: "GitHub → Vercel"
    preamble: "Deployment used to be hard. With a repo connected to Vercel, it's a setup screen and a push. Try writing the prompt that walks you through it before you copy."
    body: |
      I have a GitHub repo with my project. I want to deploy it to [Vercel / Netlify] so every push to main goes live. Walk me through the setup, step by step. After it's connected, show me how to add an environment variable for my API key, and confirm it's available to the deployed app. Don't assume any prior deploy experience. After each step, stop and let me confirm before moving on.
  - title: "Meta and OG"
    preamble: "When you share your URL, what does it look like in chat preview? That's the OG image. Worth a few minutes of attention."
    body: |
      Generate the meta tags for my project: title, description, OG image tags, favicon, theme color, anything else worth having. The OG image should match my visual direction "[name]" and feel like the project, not a stock template — describe what it should show, and I'll generate the image separately. Tell me exactly where each piece of HTML goes, and how to test that the OG renders correctly in chat previews.
  - title: "The five-minute story"
    preamble: "What's the talk you'd give if you had to convince a designer who's never heard of your project? Try writing the structure yourself before you copy."
    body: |
      I'm presenting my project for 5 minutes to other designers. Help me write the talk. Structure: 30 seconds on the user, 60 seconds on the problem, 90 seconds on the build, 60 seconds on what surprised me, 30 seconds on what's next. For each block, write the actual sentences I should say. Don't use marketing language. I'm talking to other designers, not selling to clients.
outputs:
  - "A live URL"
  - "Push-to-deploy active (GitHub integration)"
  - "Meta and OG in place"
  - "A 5-minute talk delivered"
watch:
  - "Polish gets squeezed. Make sure W11's fix list is real and short."
  - "Bugs at this stage? Ship anyway. v1 lives, v2 fixes."
  - "Don't let people apologise for unfinished pieces. Ship is ship."
  - "Schedule a four-week follow-up. What stuck matters more than what was learned."
