---
num: "12"
title: "Polish. Ship. Show."
phase: "Ship"
principle: "Ship beats perfect. v1 goes live, v2 fixes what's left. A real thing in the world teaches you more than a perfect thing on your laptop."
capability: "Polish discipline; deploy via GitHub integration; presentation thinking"
aim: "Run your must-fix list, get a live URL, and tell the room what you made."
toolkit: "Cowork / Claude Code · Vercel or Netlify"
tools:
  - Cowork / Claude Code
  - Vercel
flow:
  - Fix the must-haves
  - Deploy live
  - Tell the story
exercise:
  - "Polish: fixes only, from last week's must-fix list."
  - "Connect the repo to Vercel and get a live URL, with the share preview set."
  - "Write and give a five-minute show and tell."
prompts:
  - title: "Last polish pass"
    tool: "Cowork / Claude Code"
    does: "Run the three must-fixes from last week. Nothing past the 25-minute mark, no new features."
    preamble: "What does your project still need? The honest answer is shorter than the panicked one."
    body: |
      I have 25 minutes for final polish. Walk through my project [link, or point to the files] and give me my three must-fixes from last week's review, ranked by impact per minute. Stop me if I drift toward features. End the list at the 25-minute mark, and make the last item the one I'd be most embarrassed to ship without.
    gives: "The must-fix list done, and a hard stop at 25 minutes."
  - title: "Deploy, and make the preview yours"
    tool: "Vercel / Netlify"
    does: "Connect your repo so every push to main goes live, then make the share preview look like your project."
    preamble: "Deployment used to be hard. With a repo connected, it isn't. What's the one thing you're unsure of? Ask it."
    body: |
      I have a GitHub repo with my project. I want to deploy it to [Vercel / Netlify] so every push to main goes live. Walk me through the setup step by step, and stop after each step so I can confirm. If I have an API key, show me how to add it as an environment variable and confirm the deployed app can see it.

      Then generate my meta tags: title, description, OG image tags, favicon, theme colour. The OG image should match my [project]-design skill and feel like the project, not a template. Tell me where each tag goes and how to test that the preview renders in a chat.
    note: "Bugs at this stage? Ship anyway. v1 lives, v2 fixes."
    gives: "A live URL, push-to-deploy, and a share preview that looks like your project."
  - title: "The five-minute story"
    tool: "Claude chat"
    does: "Write the five-minute talk. Plain language, for designers, not a client pitch."
    preamble: "What's the talk you'd give to a designer who's never heard of your project? Try the structure yourself first."
    body: |
      I'm presenting my project for five minutes to other designers. Help me write the talk. Structure: 30 seconds on the user, 60 on the problem, 90 on the build, 60 on what surprised me, 30 on what's next. For each block, write the actual sentences I'd say. No marketing language. I'm talking to other designers, not selling to clients.
    gives: "A talk written in your words, for the room."
outputs:
  - "Must-fixes done."
  - "A live URL with push-to-deploy."
  - "Meta and OG in place."
  - "A five-minute talk delivered."
watch:
  - "Polish gets squeezed. Keep last week's fix list real and short."
  - "Don't apologise for unfinished pieces. Ship is ship."
  - "Schedule a follow-up a month out. What stuck matters more than what was learned."
---
