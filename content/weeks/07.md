  - "Strict: one section per prompt"
  - "Every prompt includes your design reference (Figma screenshot or Claude Design section export) + tokens + banned moves"

prompts:
  - title: "Section-by-section build"
    preamble: "What would happen if you asked Claude for the whole page at once versus one section at a time? Try writing the per-section prompt yourself first."
    body: |
      [Attach screenshot of just this section — from Figma or from your Claude Design export]

      Build the [section name] section. Constraints:
      - Use my tokens [paste]
      - Match the design reference closely. Proportions, spacing, type, alignment.
      - Visual direction "[name]"
      - Banned moves: [paste]
      - Just this section. Don’t build the rest.

      Don’t normalise unusual choices. If something looks unusual in the reference, treat it as intentional.
  - title: "Catching stock-UI drift"
    preamble: "Claude drifts toward safe choices. Where in your project would you spot drift first: corners, colors, spacing, type, shadows?"
    body: |
      Looking at what you’ve built so far, has anything drifted toward generic SaaS aesthetics? Check: rounded corners that shouldn’t be there, colors reverted to safer ones, type that’s gotten more default, added shadows or gradients I didn’t ask for, padding that’s gotten more uniform. List the drift with line numbers, and patch only those.
  - title: "Stop. Reread the design reference."
    preamble: "When Claude’s on autopilot, the fastest way to reset is to force it to look at your reference again."
    body: |
      Before you make the next change, look again at the design reference I gave you. Tell me three specific things in it you think you’ve matched. Then three you’re not sure about. Don’t change anything yet. I’ll tell you what to fix.
outputs:
  - "A polished main view that matches your design reference"
  - "Visible character. Feels like the designer’s work, not stock SaaS."
