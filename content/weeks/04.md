---
num: "04"
title: "Plan the whole thing. Wireframes and all."
phase: "Architect"
capability: "Sitemap construction; low-fi wireframing; state mapping at app level; content planning per screen"
aim: "What are all the screens your app needs? Without a complete map, you'll build the home screen beautifully and panic about the rest in W09."
toolkit: "Opus in Claude.ai chat. Architecture decisions ripple through every later session; depth pays back."
exercise:
  - "Build the sitemap. Every screen, how they connect."
  - "Wireframe each screen in low fidelity. Paper, Figma, Whimsical — the tool doesn't matter."
  - "Map states for every screen"
  - "Write real content for every screen. No lorem."
prompts:
  - title: "Sitemap from the brief"
    preamble: "Before you copy: list every screen your app needs from memory. Then check Claude against your list. Where do you disagree?"
    body: |
      Take my project brief [paste]. Walk me through every distinct screen this app needs. For each: name, one-line purpose, what data it shows or collects, what actions are possible. Map the connections — how does the user get from each screen to each other screen. End with a visual sitemap I can sketch from. Push back on screens that feel like dead weight.
  - title: "Wireframe with words"
    preamble: "Before Figma, before code, can you describe each screen out loud? What sits where, what's the hierarchy. If you can't say it, you can't design it."
    body: |
      For [screen name], describe a low-fidelity wireframe in words. Where does each element sit on the screen — top, left, center, bottom-right? What's the rough hierarchy? What's the primary action? Don't worry about visuals or styling. Just the structural skeleton. Treat it like the wireframe a designer would sketch on a napkin.
  - title: "State map per screen"
    preamble: "What does your app look like at the moment a brand-new user opens it? With no data, no progress, nothing. That's the state most often forgotten."
    body: |
      For [screen name], list every state it can be in: default, loading, empty (no data yet), error, success, partial, hovered, focused, transitioning. For each: what should be visible, what should be hidden, how does the user move between states. Mark which states are most likely to be skipped and tell me why I shouldn't skip them.
  - title: "Content map per screen"
    preamble: "Lorem ipsum hides bad design. If you can't write the real button label, the screen probably isn't fully thought-through."
    body: |
      For [screen name], list every piece of content shown. For each: where does the content come from (user input, API call, hardcoded, AI-generated), and what's the minimum content needed for the screen to be useful. If I'm missing content I haven't decided, ask me one question at a time before writing it.
outputs:
  - "One-page sitemap"
  - "Low-fi wireframes for every screen"
  - "State map per screen"
  - "Content map per screen"
watch:
  - "Designers love spending the whole hour on the prettiest screen. Force coverage of every screen, ugly ones included."
  - "Sitemap shrinks under questioning. Let it shrink."
  - "Real content beats lorem. Insist on it."
