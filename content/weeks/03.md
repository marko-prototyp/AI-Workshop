title: "Set the visual direction."
phase: "Discover → Define"
capability: "Image generation with references; building anti-stock guardrails"
aim: "Articulate visual taste in words specific enough that Claude can't return five tasteful, identical answers. The banned-moves list you write today gets referenced in every future prompt."
toolkit: "Sonnet in Claude.ai chat. Direction-setting is creative more than analytical; speed of iteration wins."
exercise:
  - "Explore 3 distinct visual directions for your project"
  - "Build a banned-moves list of 10 to 15 specific items"
prompts:
  - title: "Style brainstorm with references"
    preamble: "If you asked Claude for visual directions and got five tasteful answers that all looked similar, what was missing? Try writing the prompt yourself before you copy."
    body: |
      My project is [brief]. Brainstorm 3 distinct visual directions. For each, give me: a 2-word name, the design movements it draws on, 5 concrete style descriptors, a color palette in words, 3 designers or studios whose work fits, and 3 things this direction would NOT do.
  - title: "The banned moves list"
    preamble: "What's easier to name: what you want your project to look like, or what you'd hate it to look like? The hate list is usually sharper."
    body: |
      Based on my chosen direction "[name]" and the references I love, draft a "banned moves" list — specific design moves I will not allow. Cover: shapes, color moves, type behaviors, interaction cliches, generic words from copy. Aim for 10 to 15 specific bans. Be concrete: not "no generic icons" but "no rounded square icons in pastel boxes."
outputs:
  - "Visual direction document"
  - "6 to 10 reference images"
  - "Banned-moves list of 10 to 15 specific items"
watch:
  - "Force people to try three directions, not pick the first one"
  - "The banned-moves list will feel pedantic now. It becomes essential by W07."
  - "Direction is settled here. Don't reopen it in later sessions."
