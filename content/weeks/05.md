capability: "Rapid HTML prototyping with Claude; testing flow before craft; iterating on functionality, content, features"
aim: "Before you spend hours in Figma comping every screen, build the cheap version. Click through it. Does the concept hold? What didn't you expect?"
toolkit: "Sonnet in Claude.ai chat with Artifacts. Smoke test wants speed over depth; the artifact lives inside the chat."
exercise:
  - "Build the single-file artifact. Every screen reachable, fake data, minimal styling."
  - "Click through it as the real user"
  - "Replace lorem with real content"
  - "Update W04 wireframes and state map with what you learned"
prompts:
  - title: "Build the concept artifact"
    preamble: "What's the smallest version of your app where every screen exists and the user can navigate between them? That's what you're asking Claude to build. Resist asking for polish."
    body: |
      I have a sitemap and wireframes for [project]. Build the smallest possible HTML artifact in a single file that lets me click through every screen and feel the concept. Use: semantic markup, minimal styling (just enough to read hierarchy), fake data where real data would go, working navigation between screens. No polish. No decoration. This is a smoke test. After it's built, list three things you weren't sure about and ask me to confirm before finalising.
  - title: "Try the flow as the real user"
    preamble: "Click through it yourself first. Where does it get awkward? Where do you wish a screen existed that doesn't? Then have Claude do the same."
    body: |
      Walk through my artifact as if you were [target user — name them specifically: the actual person this is for, what they want from it, the device they'll use]. Tell me where the flow breaks down, where you'd get lost, where you'd want to do something that isn't possible yet. Don't critique the design. Critique the experience. Be specific.
  - title: "Sharpen the content"
    preamble: "If you can write real content for every screen, the design is half done. If you can't, your screen isn't fully thought-through yet."
    body: |
      Look at every screen of my artifact. The placeholder content is fake. Help me write real content — actual button labels, real headlines, real microcopy. Match my visual direction "[name]" and banned-words list [paste]. If I'm missing content I haven't decided on, ask me a specific question about it before writing it. One question at a time.
  - title: "What did you discover?"
    preamble: "What surprised you when you clicked through? That's the wireframe update."
    body: |
      Compare the W04 wireframes and state map I gave you to what we just built. What's missing? What's wrong? What states didn't I plan for that the build needed? Update the wireframes and state map to match what we now know works. Be honest — flag the screens that turned out worse than expected.
outputs:
  - "One clickable HTML artifact, single file, end-to-end"
  - "Real content written for every screen"
  - "Updated wireframes and state map reflecting reality"
watch:
  - "It's a smoke test. Don't let people polish. If they're choosing fonts, pull them back."
  - "The discoveries here save 4 weeks of building the wrong thing later."
  - "Real content. Always real content. Lorem ipsum is a lie."
