---
num: "09"
title: "Polish. Make it feel finished."
phase: "Make"
principle: "Polish is subtraction. Change one thing at a time, so you can see what each change did and catch what it broke."
capability: "Consistency auditing against your skill; tightening; restraint; one considered micro-interaction"
aim: "Tighten what you built. Polish is what you remove, not what you add."
toolkit: "Claude Code · your eyes"
tools:
  - Claude Code
  - Your eyes
flow:
  - Find rough edges
  - Tighten
  - Delete one thing
before: "No new screens this week. Your skill is the reference for what consistent looks like, so every fix snaps back to it."
exercise:
  - "Get a consistency audit against your skill's tokens."
  - "Tighten spacing, type, and colour one fix at a time."
  - "Delete one thing that isn't pulling its weight."
  - "Optional: add one micro-interaction that earns its place."
prompts:
  - title: "Find the rough edges"
    tool: "Claude chat"
    does: "Have Claude hunt for inconsistency against your skill's tokens. Find it before you fix it."
    preamble: "Where does your project look hand-made in the bad way: uneven spacing, drifting type, off-token colour?"
    body: |
      Audit my project for visual inconsistency. [point Code at the files, or paste the screen].

      Check it against the tokens and type scale in my [project]-design skill: spacing that isn't on the scale, type sizes that drifted, colours that aren't tokens, radii and borders that vary without reason, alignment that's slightly off. List every inconsistency with its location, grouped by type, ranked by how visible it is. Don't fix anything yet, and don't comment on what's already consistent.
    gives: "A located, ranked list of every inconsistency."
  - title: "Tighten one thing at a time"
    tool: "Claude Code"
    does: "Fix one inconsistency per change, back to the right token. Small and surgical."
    preamble: "Vague asks get vague fixes. What's the exact value that's off, and what should it be?"
    body: |
      Fix this one inconsistency: [name it, with its location]. Snap it to the right token from my [project]-design skill. Change only this. Don't touch anything else, don't re-derive tokens, don't improve nearby code. If the fix would affect other elements, tell me before you do it.
    gives: "Spacing, type, and colour back in line with your skill."
  - title: "Delete one thing"
    tool: "Claude Code"
    does: "Find what isn't pulling its weight and cut it. Polish is subtraction."
    preamble: "What's the one 'just in case' element you could remove right now and miss nothing?"
    body: |
      Look at [screen or section]. Name everything on it that isn't pulling its weight: a 'just in case' element, a decoration that adds nothing, a control nobody needs, a label that repeats another. For each, tell me what the screen loses if I remove it. Don't remove anything yet. I'll pick.
    gives: "A lighter screen. One less thing to defend."
  - title: "One motion that earns its place"
    tool: "Claude Code"
    does: "Optional. At most one considered micro-interaction. Motion that helps, not motion that shows off."
    preamble: "Does this motion help the user, or just impress you? If it's the second, skip it."
    body: |
      I want one small motion on [element]: [describe what should move and when]. Before any code: tell me whether it helps the user or just decorates. If it decorates, say so and stop. If it helps, describe it in plain English first, the trigger, what moves, the duration, the easing, then build it in vanilla CSS or JS to match my project. Keep it subtle. One interaction, not a system.
    note: "Optional. Skip it with a clear conscience."
    gives: "At most one micro-interaction that fits the project."
outputs:
  - "A consistency audit."
  - "Spacing and type tightened."
  - "One thing deleted."
  - "Optional: one considered motion."
watch:
  - "No new features this week. Polish is subtraction."
  - "Fix one thing at a time. Batched fixes hide what broke."
---
