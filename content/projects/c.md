name: "A tiny tool"
desc: "A single-purpose utility with personality. Does one thing; you'd send the URL to a friend."
bestFor: "makers who want utility"
pickIf: |
  you like making things and you want a portfolio piece that's also useful. You'd rather ship a working artifact than write about your work.
goesWrong: |
  Scope creep. The contrast checker gets a "while we're at it, let's also export palettes" feature. Then a saved-projects feature. Then accounts. By week 10 the tool is half-built and unfocused. The fix: write down what the tool ISN'T in week 1, and treat that list as load-bearing.
goodLooks: |
  It does one thing, you remember the URL three weeks later, and you'd send it to a friend. It has personality — the type, the writing, the empty state, the error state, all feel like one designer made decisions. It's the opposite of a Bootstrap-y utility page.
trap: |
  If you can't describe what it does in one sentence without "and," it's not tiny anymore. Cut until you can.

TOOL NAME
Working name. Will probably change. Keep it short and weird before
you make it short and clever.

ONE SENTENCE — WHAT IT DOES
"It [verb] [noun] [for whom/in what way]."
Example: "It generates an accessible color palette from any photo
you drop in." If you write "and" mid-sentence, cut.

WHO USES IT
Specific. "Designers picking type for a landing page" beats
"designers." The more specific the user, the more opinionated the
tool can be.

INPUTS
Every input the user provides. Image upload? Two color hexes? A
URL? Be exhaustive — undeclared inputs are how scope creeps.

OUTPUTS
Every output the user sees. A palette? A score? A downloadable
PNG? Same exhaustiveness.

THE WOW
What makes this fun, not just functional? The thing someone would
screenshot. A contrast checker is utility. A contrast checker
that grades scores like a movie review with quotes is wow.

THE PERSONALITY
Three adjectives for how the tool itself feels. Not the brand,
the tool. "Calm, technical, slightly sardonic." "Loud, fast,
playful." The personality drives type, color, microcopy, empty
states.

WHAT IT'S NOT
Three things, minimum. Specifically things you might be tempted
to add. Examples:
- It's NOT going to have user accounts or saved projects.
- It's NOT going to export to Figma in v1.
- It's NOT trying to compete with [bigger tool]. It does one
  thing they don't.
